PhD Title:

Representation Learning For Pattern Spotting And Pattern Discovery In Historical Documents.

1.

A plethora of computer vision sub-domains are involved in this project, such as transfer learning, one-shot object detection, transformer-based architectures, unsupervised learning and synthetic data generation.

This project tackles the challenging problem of detecting any arbitrary query object in a collection of historical documents, while having access to zero training data.
2.

Published a paper in ICPR 2024 titled:
Learning-Based Sub-Image Retrieval in Historical Documents.

We propose the first learning-based approach for solving pattern spotting and sub-image retrieval in historical documents.
Among the contributions is the novel OS-DETR architecture, depicted here.
Other key contributions focus on the process of generating synthetic training data as well as proposing a series of generalization techniques aimed to boost performance on real data.
